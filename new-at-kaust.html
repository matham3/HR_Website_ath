Probation Period

The probation period is a great way to integrate and support you during the first few months of employment. It is highly recommended that during the probation period you have weekly meetings with your manager to ensure that you are settling in and that expectations are being met.

Your probation period starts on the first day you begin working for the University and is 90 days long (exclusive of University public holidays and any sick leave days). It can be extended up to 180 days by written mutual agreement.

Shipment Services

Please Note: Your shipping allowance will depend on your contract.

Do you want to ship your personal effects to KAUST? Our experienced relocation advisors have made the process as straightforward as possible.

If you were eligible for a shipment when joining KAUST, you will have already received details of your allowance and assigned shipping agent in your home country. If you do not have this information, please contact your relocation advisor directly or email HR Relocation.

Shipping Allowance by Classification

Classification Single Spouse One Child Two Children Three Children
Executives and Academic Leadership KGS 400 KGS 800 KGS 900 KGS 1,000 KGS 1,100
Managers, Professionals, Faculty, Teachers and Researchers KGS 300 KGS 600 KGS 700 KGS 800 KGS 900
Support Staff KGS 200 KGS 440 KGS 500 KGS 600 KGS 700

Once you have had your shipment packed in your country of origin, the shipment will wait until your Iqama has been issued. When your Iqama is ready, the shipment will be delivered by air, land or sea.

Estimated Shipping Times:

  • 6-8 weeks by air delivery
  • 4-6 months by sea delivery
  • 7-10 days if you are already in KSA

Can I change my point of origin?

Yes, your point of origin can be changed. However, if the change results in a greater cost to KAUST than the originally stated point of origin, then the additional costs are paid by you, the employee.

What does a point of origin determine?

The University will use the point of origin to determine:

  • Air travel and shipment of personal effects' entitlement when joining KAUST
  • Annual travel (repatriation) allowance
  • Airfare and shipment of personal effects' entitlement upon separation from KAUST

If you are experiencing any difficulties with your shipment, please contact your relocation advisor HR Relocation.

Buying a Car

If you are planning to buy a new car, all the main car dealerships have showrooms in Jeddah, as well as an online presence. Once you have chosen the car you want, just schedule an appointment for an inspection and a test drive. If you decide to buy a new car, the dealership will help you with the required paperwork.

⚠️ IMPORTANT

The primary sponsored KAUST employee and/or their dependents may own a car(s) if they have a Saudi driver's license. However, it is the main sponsor who must obtain the KAUST vehicle sticker.

Dependents with a driving license are permitted to drive if they are added to the car insurance policy as a designated driver.

Driving in Saudi Arabia

Applying for a Driving License

In order to apply for a license, please make sure you have all the required documents and that you pay for the license before your test.

Documents and Information Required:

  • Original Iqama (including the primary iqama if you are a dependent)
  • Original driving license (if applicable)
  • 200 SAR for a medical check-up
  • 4 x personal passport-sized photos
  • Your blood group (e.g. O+, A-, A+ etc.)
  • The transmission type for your vehicle (manual or automatic)
  • Proof that the driving license fee has been paid through online banking (SADAD)

KAUST Vehicle Registration

To drive your car around campus, you will need to register your car with Security e-Services. As well as cars, all other motorized vehicles (golf carts, scooters etc.) are required to display an official KAUST vehicle registration plate. Vehicles with a KAUST-issued plate are not allowed on any public roads outside the KAUST campus.

⚠️ Important: Cars and motorbikes with a Saudi registration plate require a vehicle digital sticker.

Vehicle Digital Sticker Request

Requests for a vehicle digital sticker can be made via the Security e-Services Platform (VMS). To request a sticker, go to the "Vehicle Tag" section and "Create Vehicle Tag Request." Upon completion of the form, an appointment request for the installation of your digital sticker will be emailed to you.

Opening a Bank Account

To open a local bank account, you will need your Iqama and a Saudi mobile number.

When you have both your Iqama and a mobile SIM card, you can open a bank account with any Saudi bank. There are several banks in the Jeddah area.

On-Campus Banking Options:
Most KAUST employees use one of our two on-campus bank branches:

  • SNB - Located in Discovery Square
  • SAB - Located on the University Spine

Both banks have ATMs within KAUST. You are more than welcome to visit the branches before opening an account in order to find out which one suits you best. After applying, it can take a couple of days for the account to be activated.

Your monthly salary will be transferred to your local Saudi bank account. Remember that Payroll tends to close on the 15th of the month for payday on the 25th.

💡 Note: You can update your bank details directly through HR Connect.

Petty Cash - First Few Weeks

Once you have received your KAUST ID, if eligible, you can request up to SAR 5,000 as a cash advance, which will then be deducted from your first salary payment. To discuss this option and to request a cash advance, please contact your HR Relocation Advisor, who will be happy to assist you.

Important Note: If you have brought foreign currency with you, the local banks will only exchange cash for Saudi Riyals once you have obtained your Iqama.

Your ID & Documentation

There are two important IDs that you will need. The first is your Iqama (for expatriates only).

Obtaining an Iqama

An Iqama is the legal residency permit that allows expatriates to live and work in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

Upon arrival in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia on a work or dependent visa, your HR Relocation Advisor will work with you on your first day at KAUST to complete the necessary forms to apply for an Iqama.

On your first day, please make sure you bring your passport with you in order to complete the Iqama application process.

A medical check-up is required as part of the Iqama application process (except for children under 16 years of age). This is different from the medical check-up you underwent for processing your visa before coming to the Kingdom. The medical check-up for your Iqama will be carried out at a location inside the campus. The medical result will then be sent to the appropriate ministry along with all the other relevant documents to process your Iqama.

The standard processing time for an Iqama is 2-3 weeks, but this varies depending on the time of the year. Your HR Relocation Advisor will work closely with Government Affairs to make sure that you receive the Iqama as soon as possible.

Once you have obtained your Iqama you can receive your shipment, obtain a local phone SIM card and open a local bank account.

KAUST ID

Everyone over 12 years of age at KAUST is required to have a photo identification card (KAUST ID), which will be issued by Government Affairs during your first day of work. You will need your passport (with the appropriate visa) in order to process this.

Your KAUST ID is necessary to access facilities such as the library, KAUST Health services and recreational facilities. If you lose your KAUST ID, you should report this immediately to the Security Office in Building 3, Level 2.

Welcome to Saudi Arabia

Marhaba! Welcome to the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

You're stepping into a land of extraordinary contrasts—where ancient heritage meets bold Vision 2030 transformation. From the historic streets of Diriyah to the futuristic skyline of Riyadh, Saudi Arabia offers a journey through time and culture unlike anywhere else.

Essential Information for Newcomers:

  • Language: Arabic is the official language, though English is widely spoken in business and at KAUST.
  • Culture & Hospitality: Experience the legendary warmth of Saudi hospitality. Respect for tradition and modesty are valued alongside openness to global perspectives.
  • Climate: Hot, sunny summers (May-September) and mild, pleasant winters. Perfect weather for Red Sea adventures!
  • Weekend: Friday and Saturday. The work week runs Sunday through Thursday.
  • Explore: UNESCO World Heritage Sites, pristine beaches, vast deserts, modern cities, and vibrant cultural festivals await you.

KAUST's relocation team will guide you through settling in—from your Iqama (residence permit) to understanding local customs. You're joining a thriving international community in one of the world's most dynamic regions.

KAUST Culture & Values

Welcome to a truly multicultural community. At KAUST, we celebrate diversity and foster an environment where every member can express their unique culture and identity while maintaining mutual respect and understanding.

Our vibrant community brings together individuals from over 100 nationalities, each contributing to the rich tapestry of life at KAUST. We believe that this diversity is our greatest strength, driving innovation and creating a dynamic environment where different perspectives come together to solve global challenges.

Living the KAUST Code of Conduct
To ensure everyone thrives in our multicultural campus, we uphold the KAUST Code of Conduct—a framework designed to promote a safe, productive, healthy, and pleasant work and social environment. The Code guides behaviors that demonstrate respect for colleagues and residents, while fostering collaboration and excellence.

All members of the University community—faculty, staff, students, partners, contractors, and visitors—are expected to read, understand, and adhere to the Code of Conduct. It serves as our shared commitment to creating a workplace where everyone can perform at their best.

A Shared Responsibility
As an employee, you play a vital role in maintaining our community standards. Please share the Code of Conduct with all family members and personal visitors to help everyone understand and respect the environment we've built together.

By embracing these values, you contribute to making KAUST a place where innovation flourishes, relationships thrive, and every individual is valued for their unique contributions to our global community.
